Our Problem Statement

Low agricultural productivity, driven by climate change, affects 70% of Zimbabwe's rural population who depend on smallholder farming. This issue, reported by the Zimbabwe National Statistics Agency, is prevalent in rural areas with limited access to irrigation and technology, peaking during the dry season (May-October). The root causes include insufficient technology, lack of climate-resilient practices, and inadequate government support. This critical issue jeopardizes food security, economic growth, and sustainable development. By 2028, we aim to boost agricultural productivity by 30% among 10,000 smallholder farmers in Zimbabwe.

Our Mission

The Unified Goals Lab harnesses cutting-edge technology to revolutionize agriculture, strengthen food security, and combat climate change. By integrating IoT, AI, and precision farming, we empower farmers with real-time data, enabling them to maximize yields, minimize losses, and drive sustainable agricultural growth across Africa.
